Who Am I?

I'm Jai — a self-taught developer transitioning into tech from a background in warehousing and hands-on operations. I discovered a passion for web development while building my own online CV using HTML and CSS, which quickly grew into a deeper journey through programming, design, and real-world problem solving.

With a practical mindset and strong work ethic, I approach development as both a craft and a tool to create accessible, meaningful solutions. I enjoy learning new technologies, building functional tools, and applying my skills to projects that have real impact.

From Warehouse to Web Development

My early work experience taught me discipline, teamwork, and efficiency — qualities I now apply to programming every day. I first began coding on my phone, building a CV from scratch using HTML and CSS. That project led to a tech interview with a major logistics company. I didn’t get the job — there were over 200 applicants — but the hiring manager was impressed, and the experience taught me something important: in tech, skills can speak louder than formal education.

Since then, I’ve invested in a second-hand HP laptop and fully committed to learning. I’ve been using AI as a personal mentor, guiding me through concepts and helping me grow in the absence of formal training. The best feeling I’ve found is spending hours writing, breaking, fixing, and refining code — and finally watching it work. That moment makes every challenge worth it.
