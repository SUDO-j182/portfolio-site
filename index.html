Who Am I?

I'm Jai, a self-taught developer with a background in warehouse and factory work. Since I was a kid, I’ve been curious about how computers and software work. That interest stuck with me, and now I’m learning to turn it into a career.

I first got into web development by building a basic CV using HTML and CSS on my phone. That project led to an interview with a major company, and even though I didn’t get the role, it gave me the push I needed to take things further. Every project I’ve worked on since then has helped me grow.

From Warehouse to Web Development

Working in fast-paced jobs taught me how to stay consistent, solve problems, and work well in a team. I bring the same mindset to coding — patient, focused, and always trying to get better. I’ve been learning independently using free resources and AI tools, writing code, breaking it, fixing it, and learning from the process.

I now use a second-hand laptop and free online tools to teach myself everything I can. Watching a project finally work after hours of trial and error is the best feeling — and the main reason I keep going.

Over the Past Year, I’ve Built:

  • Portfolio Website – Built with HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. Shows my progress and updates as I learn.
  • Password Generator – Helped me learn logic, user input, and randomisation in JavaScript.
