Who Am I?

I'm Jai — a self-taught developer transitioning into tech from a background in warehousing. Diagnosed with ADHD at age 8, I struggled in traditional learning environments, but now recognize it as a strength: a unique lens that drives my focus, resilience, and creative thinking. What started out as a way to stand out from other tech applicants in the IT industry, an online CV/resume developed entirely with HTML and CSS quickly spiraled into a journey of continuous learning in web development, programming, and cloud computing. I bring with me a practical mindset, a love for solving problems, and a desire to build technology that serves people and solves real problems.

From Warehouse to Web Development

My early work history taught me discipline, teamwork, and efficiency — all qualities I now apply to programming. I first started coding on my phone, using html and css to craft a CV that got me a tech interview at a massive logistics organization. Although i didnt get the job due to 200 potentially more suitable candidates, the hiring manager was impressed and that experience taught me that employers value skills more than education at least to a certain degree (no pun intended). Since then i loaned the money to buy a second hand HP laptop and got to work learning all i could, i have been using AI as a teacher or mentor in place of professional guidance. The best feeling ive ever had is when ive spent hours writing code, tweaking it, breaking it, fixing it and understanding it then it finally works as intended. That feeling alone is worth all the trouble in the world in my opinion.

Over the past year, I’ve built:

  • Portfolio Website – Built using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. Continuously updated as my skills improve.
  • Password Generator – Taught me core JavaScript logic and basic UX thinking.
  • Drawing Tool – Helped me deepen my DOM and event handling skills.
